#  How to use Veridian

Veridian is a prompt-based AI marking system. You copy the prompt
into Claude, paste your answer, and it marks it to examiner standard.

---

## Step 1 — Choose your prompt

| Subject | Paper | File to use |
|---|---|---|
| Economics | WEC12/01 — Paper 2 | `LITE/VERIDIAN_V6_E_LITE.md` |
| Business Studies | WBS12/01 — Paper 2 | `LITE/VERIDIAN_V6_B_LITE.md` |

Use the LITE `.md` files — they are formatted for direct use with Claude.

---

## Step 2 — Open Claude (Deepseek + Grok often give better answers)

Go to [claude.ai](https://claude.ai) and start a new conversation.

---

## Step 3 — Paste the prompt

Open the relevant `.md` file on GitHub.
Click **Raw** (top right of the file).
Select all → copy → paste into Claude.

---

## Step 4 — Paste your answer

Directly after the prompt, paste your answer in this format:

```
QUESTION: [paste the question here]
MARKS AVAILABLE: [e.g. 20]
MY ANSWER: [paste your full answer here]
```

---

## Step 5 — Read your feedback

Veridian will return:
- Your mark with level justification
- Exactly which AO marks were lost and why
- A UMS estimate (Economics only)
- A rewrite plan to move your answer up a grade

---

## Tips

- Always include the question — Veridian needs it to assess relevance
- For diagram questions, describe your diagram in words
- One question per conversation for best results
- If you want harsher feedback, add: `Mark in ultra-harsh mode`

---

## Disclaimer

Veridian is for formative practice only. Marks are estimates based on
official Pearson mark schemes and are not a guarantee of exam performance.
