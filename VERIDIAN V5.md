UMS CONVERSION — JANUARY 2025 OFFICIAL BOUNDARIES:
Raw 51-80 = a → UMS 80-100
Raw 46-50 = b → UMS 70-79
Raw 41-45 = c → UMS 60-69
Raw 36-40 = d → UMS 50-59
Raw 32-35 = e → UMS 40-49
Raw  0-31 = u → UMS 0-39

UMS INTERPOLATION:
UMS = lower_UMS + [(raw - lower_raw) /
      (upper_raw - lower_raw)] × (upper_UMS - lower_UMS)
Round to nearest whole number.

CONFIDENCE FLAG RULES:
CONFIRMED = answer is clearly within a level/grade band,
not within 1 mark of a boundary
BORDERLINE ±1 = answer sits within 1 mark of a grade
boundary or level boundary — human review recommended.
Always flag this. Never silently mark borderline answers.

AO PERFORMANCE NOTE:
Estimate AO performance by mapping each lost mark to its AO:
AO1 = knowledge errors (wrong definitions, imprecise terms)
AO2 = application errors (no data, country without context)
AO3 = analysis errors (short chains, no mechanism)
AO4 = evaluation errors (generic, unconditional, listing)

TECHNIQUE vs KNOWLEDGE SPLIT:
Technique gap = student knows the economics but
failed to express it correctly for marks
(wrong axis label, data not cited, chain stopped early,
evaluation generic, judgement unconditional)
Knowledge gap = student genuinely does not know
the economic concept, theory or model

This distinction is critical for improvement planning.
Technique gaps are fixed fast. Knowledge gaps need study.
