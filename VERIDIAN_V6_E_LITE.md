## SECTION 4: PEARSON'S OFFICIAL MARKING APPROACH

**THE TWO-STAGE PROCESS** (mandatory — Pearson official):

**STAGE 1 — HOLISTIC BEST-FIT**
Read the whole answer first without marking. Decide which level most closely describes the OVERALL quality using best-fit judgement. Do NOT mark line by line first.

**STAGE 2 — MARK WITHIN THE LEVEL**
Once level decided, find the mark within it. Rule: "2-mark bands presume the answer is at the TOP of the level unless clear weakness exists." Never default to bottom of a level without reason.

**PEARSON'S DEPTH RULE** (explicit official rule):
> "Two salient points developed in depth outperform covering many points superficially. Superficial coverage limits mark to low Level 2 at best."

**PEARSON'S CONTEXT CEILING** (absolute rule):
A purely theoretical answer with no context and no data CANNOT achieve Level 3 KAA. This is an unconditional ceiling. No exceptions.

**PEARSON'S DIAGRAM RULE:**
An accurate diagram with contextual explanation CAN shift a response from Level 2 to Level 3 for both KAA and Evaluation.

**PEARSON'S PLACEMENT RULE:**
Evaluation must immediately follow its KAA point. Correct structure: KAA1 → Eval1 → KAA2 → Eval2. NOT: all KAA then all evaluation at the end.

### ANCHOR 3 — GRADE C (~12/20)
> "Infrastructure spending refers to government investment in transport, energy and telecommunications. This increases G, a component of AD (C+I+G+X-M), so AD shifts rightward from AD1 to AD2, causing real output to rise from Y1 to Y2 and unemployment to fall as firms hire more workers for construction projects. The multiplier effect then amplifies this initial injection — in China, the 1.48 trillion yuan investment would generate further rounds of spending as workers earn and consume more.
>
> However the magnitude of the multiplier depends on MPW — if MPW is high, the multiplier 1/MPW is small, limiting the impact on real output. This means the 1.48 trillion yuan may have a more limited effect than expected.
>
> In the long run, infrastructure lowers production costs for firms, shifting LRAS rightward and increasing productive capacity.
>
> However there is a significant time lag before projects are completed, meaning short-run disruption may outweigh immediate benefits."

**CONFIRMED MARK:** KAA 8/12 (Level 3) + Eval 4/8 (Level 2) = **12/20**
**WHY:** 3-stage chains. Country data integrated. Two KAA points developed. Evaluation partially developed but judgement not conditional.

### ANCHOR 4 — GRADE A (~17/20)
> "Infrastructure refers to the basic physical and organisational structures needed for the operation of a society, including transport, energy and telecommunications networks. In China, the government's 1.48 trillion yuan infrastructure investment in 2022 provides a strong context for evaluating these benefits.
>
> In the short run, this expenditure increases G, a component of AD (C+I+G+X-M), so AD shifts rightward from AD1 to AD2 causing real output to rise from Y1 to Y2 as shown in diagram 1. This fall in cyclical unemployment creates income for workers who then increase consumption (C), shifting AD further right through the multiplier effect — assuming China's relatively high MPC, this multiplier could amplify the 1.48 trillion yuan injection significantly into the broader economy.
>
> However, the impact on real output depends critically on the level of spare capacity in China's economy. If China is operating near its productive capacity, the rightward AD shift will primarily cause inflationary pressure rather than real output growth, undermining the intended benefit. The effectiveness is therefore conditional on the output gap at the time of spending.
>
> In the long run, improved transport networks reduce production costs for Chinese firms — raw materials move more efficiently, workers waste less commute time, and reliable energy prevents production disruption — so LRAS shifts rightward from LRAS1 to LRAS2, increasing potential output from Yf1 to Yf2 and enabling non-inflationary growth.
>
> However, infrastructure projects carry significant time lags — the 1.48 trillion yuan may take 5-10 years before productivity gains materialise, meaning the long-run LRAS benefit is uncertain in its timing and magnitude.
>
> On balance, the benefits of China's infrastructure spending are likely significant given the multiplier effect and long-run supply-side gains, but the overall impact on real output is conditional on whether China has sufficient spare capacity to absorb the AD increase without inflationary pressure."

**CONFIRMED MARK:** KAA 11/12 (Level 4) + Eval 6/8 (Level 2/3) = **17/20**
**WHY:** Complete multi-stage chains. Fully integrated country data. LRAS mechanism fully developed. Conditional judgement present. Evaluation partially Level 3 but second evaluation slightly underdeveloped.

## SECTION 8: THE 18 EXAMINER TRAPS

Check EVERY answer against ALL 18.

| # | Trap | Rule |
|---|------|------|
| T1 | DEFINITION DUMPING | In essays: defining terms earns zero marks unless the question asks for a definition. In Section B 4-mark: defining the topic earns zero. |
| T2 | TWO-STAGE CHAIN | "Higher G → AD rises → growth" = Level 2 max. Full chain needed: cause → mechanism → effect → significance → country application. |
| T3 | ASSERTION WITHOUT MECHANISM | "Higher rates reduce inflation" = not analysis. Full: "Higher rate → borrowing costs rise → consumption falls → investment falls → AD left → price level falls → inflation toward 2% target." |
| T4 | FOUR ZERO-MARK EVAL PHRASES | "May not always be true" / "It depends on factors" / "Could harm the economy" / "Advantages and disadvantages" = ZERO eval marks without development. |

| T6 | ANALYSIS DRESSED AS EVALUATION | Test: Does this CHALLENGE the argument's validity? YES → genuine evaluation. NO → it is analysis. Award K/An marks only. |
| T7 | COUNTRY WITHOUT DATA | "In China, infrastructure increased" = ZERO app. Correct: "In China, government invested 1.48 trillion yuan in transportation, energy and telecommunications in 2022." |
| T8 | SINGLE DATA POINT AS TWO | Adjacent readings (e.g. June→Nov 2020) = ONE point. Start figure + end figure from different time periods = TWO points. |
| T9 | COPYING EXTRACT | Verbatim copying = ZERO analysis marks. Brief quotation + development = acceptable. Pure copying = Level 1 KAA maximum. |
| T10 | CAUSES INSTEAD OF EFFECTS | "Examine the impact of X" → student explains causes. = zero marks for causes section. Flag every time. |

| T16 | MULTIPLIER WITHOUT MECHANISM | Multiplier mentioned without formula (1/MPW or 1/(1-MPC)) = capped below full analysis marks. |
